Chapter X: Resting and Healing

The world is cruel, and the apocalypse is even worse. Suffering a slip or a scratch can spell death in a world where disease is rampant and the hospitals abandoned. Suffering damage is nearly impossible and is always dangerous. Survivors must traverse the land of the dead carefully.

Defending Against Damage

A characterâ€™s defenses help prevent incoming damage, whether from an attack or from the hostile environment. All damage inflicted has a type, no matter the source. Attacks will always specify which type of damage is being dealt, while other types of damage are specified by the GM. For example, if a character fails a Prowess(Acrobatics) check while jumping from a fire escape, the GM may inflict bludgeoning damage due to the fall. A character with defense against bludgeoning would then be able to reduce or prevent some of that damage.
