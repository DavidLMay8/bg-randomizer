Chapter X: Encounters

Initiating Encounters

The GM may make an Encounter check as long as at least one of the following requirements are met:

  • Two hours have passed since the last encounter.
  • Survivors enter a new location.
  • Pay ⚠️10 - OL.
  • It is deemed narratively feasible.
  • Players spend too long deliberating.

After the requirement for an Encounter check has been met, the GM will make an Encounter check with a dice pool of 6🟩. 1s do not generate 🔴, and the GM may force successes by spending ⚠ equal to the difference. An additional 🟢 per OL is generated.

Survivor Response

After the GM has finished the Encounter check, the survivors may make a Stealth save throw against it (see Save Throws below). The survivor with the lowest Stealth score must be the one to make the check as they pose the highest risk of getting the group caught, though because the entire group is involved in the check, the performing survivor still benefits from the additional 🟩 as the other survivors assist. Each 🟢 generated by the save throw reduces 🟢 generated by the Encounter check. If the Encounter is reduced to zero 🟢, it may be avoided entirely.

Note: The GM should always roll their dice behind a GM screen. They do not need to disclose the amount of 🟢 generated by the Encounter check before survivors respond. This can be useful if the GM still wishes to have the Encounter happen, even if the check is reduced to zero 🟢 (use discretion and don’t always overrule the players).
